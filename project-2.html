Mobile Checkout Redesign

Overview

Streamlining the end-to-end checkout experience to improve conversion on mobile.

The checkout flow is the most critical path in the product. Every unnecessary step, unclear label, or moment of hesitation is a potential drop-off. This project audited and redesigned the full checkout journey — from bag review to order confirmation — with a focus on speed and confidence.

Reduce steps

Fewer screens between bag and order placed.

Surface trust signals

Make payment and address entry feel safe and familiar.

Handle errors gracefully

Recover from failures without losing the whole order.

Problem

Checkout abandonment was highest on mobile, and growing.

Analytics showed that over 30% of users who reached checkout did not complete their order on mobile — significantly higher than web. Exit surveys and session recordings pointed to three causes: confusion around delivery address entry, fees surfacing too late, and a payment flow that felt unfamiliar on native platforms.

The existing design had been patched incrementally for years and lacked cohesion. Different screens used different patterns for the same interactions.

Research

Session recordings and usability testing pinpointed the exact moments of failure.

I ran five moderated usability sessions with regular customers across different devices and OS versions. Combined with quantitative data, I mapped a clear drop-off funnel — with the address confirmation screen and the payment summary being the two biggest friction points.

5 usability sessions

Moderated, across iOS and Android users

2 critical drop-off points

Address confirmation and payment summary

30%+ abandonment

On mobile vs. 18% on web at time of audit

Solution

A consolidated three-step checkout with fees visible from the start.

The redesign collapsed the previous six-screen flow into three: bag review, delivery details, and payment. Fees were surfaced immediately — no surprises at the payment step. Address entry adopted a map-confirmation pattern familiar from competitor apps, and payment used the platform's native sheet to leverage existing trust.

I ran two rounds of prototype testing before handoff, validating the new flow reduced time-to-order-placed by an average of 40 seconds.

Outcome

Mobile checkout conversion increased by 7 percentage points post-launch.

The redesign shipped in phases with an A/B test running for four weeks across the UK market. The new flow outperformed the control on conversion rate, time-to-complete, and post-order satisfaction. It was rolled out globally over the following quarter.
