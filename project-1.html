Shop+Pay: Substitutions

Overview

Three options at checkout — giving customers the right amount of control, without adding friction.

Grocery shopping is personal. The brand you buy, the ripeness you prefer, the things you'd never swap. When we built Shop+Pay, out-of-stock items were inevitable. Substitutions gave customers a moment at checkout to decide how they want to handle it — with best match selected by default, so most customers don't have to do anything at all.

Problem

12% of items were going out of stock with no structured way for customers to handle it.

Before Substitutions launched, 12% of items in Shop+Pay orders were going out of stock with no structured way to resolve it. Couriers were left making judgment calls, customers were caught off guard, and the experience felt unpredictable. For a product built around trust — handing off your grocery list to a stranger — unpredictability was a problem we couldn't leave unsolved.

Research

Customers wanted control — and they were willing to take a moment upfront to get it.

Grocery shopping carries a level of personal preference that restaurant orders simply don't. To design the right solution, we needed to understand how much control customers actually wanted — and whether they'd use it.

We conducted moderated interviews to understand how customers thought about item substitutions — what felt acceptable, what felt intrusive, and where they wanted to be involved. Those findings were then validated at scale through surveys, ensuring the themes we heard in interviews weren't outliers. Finally, we brought the proposed options back to couriers to confirm the experience would work for both sides of the transaction — the choices customers make have a direct impact on how couriers do their job.

45% of customers preferred to set substitution preferences at checkout rather than deal with it mid-order.

Set it and forget it — customers said the ability to set preferences upfront made the overall experience feel less anxious.

Four themes emerged consistently across every conversation:

Control

Customers wanted to make the call themselves — not leave it to chance.

Transparency

They wanted to know what was happening with their order, not be surprised after the fact.

Choice

Different customers wanted different levels of involvement — the experience needed to support all of them.

Ease

Whatever the solution, it couldn't feel like extra work.

Getting Organized

The Process

Challenge

A decision at checkout about a problem that may never happen.

The substitution moment sits at an uncomfortable intersection — customers are being asked at checkout to think through what happens if an item goes out of stock during a shop that hasn't started yet. Ask for too much and it feels overwhelming. Ask for too little and customers feel out of control when something does go wrong. The experience had to feel light and optional while still giving customers who wanted it a meaningful way to set their preferences.

"I don't want to grocery shop twice"

Solution

Three options at checkout. Best match by default.

At checkout, customers see a substitution preference attached to each item in their cart. The selection is optional — best match is pre-selected so customers who don't want to engage don't have to.

Best match (default)

The courier sends the customer real-time substitution suggestions while shopping. The customer approves or rejects each one before the item goes in the cart.

Choose a replacement now

The customer picks a specific backup item at checkout. If their original is out of stock, the courier swaps it automatically — no interruption needed.

Refund

If the item is out of stock, the customer is refunded. No replacement, no message — a clean resolution.

The Experience

Customer and courier screens reflect each other — what a customer chooses shapes exactly what the courier sees.

Option 1: Best match

Customer experience

At checkout, best match is pre-selected on each cart item. Customers who want more control can tap to change it, but most will move on without a second thought. If an item goes out of stock during the shop, the customer receives a notification with options the courier has sent from the store. They approve or reject directly from the notification — no need to open the app.

Courier experience

When a courier marks an item as out of stock, they see that the customer has best match selected. Previously, couriers had to manually search the store for alternatives themselves — a time-consuming process that contributed to a ~50% substitution rejection rate. Couriers were guessing, and customers were feeling it.

To address this, we're introducing AI-powered substitution recommendations directly into the picking flow. When an item goes out of stock, couriers receive a curated list of five smart suggestions — filtered by budget, inventory availability, dietary restrictions, and proximity in the store — so the right alternatives surface instantly, without the manual search.

  • 5 AI-generated suggestions per out-of-stock item
  • Budget-aware and inventory-accurate filtering
  • Dietary compliance and restricted item safety — no alcohol or cannabis substitutions
  • Proximity-prioritized to keep picking fast

The result: less guesswork for couriers, better suggestions for customers, and a direct path to improving the substitution acceptance rate and overall fill rate. This also lays the foundational AI infrastructure for future recommendation features across the Shop+Pay experience.

During shop

As the courier shops, the customer receives a push notification for each out-of-stock item. The notification surfaces the courier's suggested substitution and gives the customer a single tap to approve or reject — no need to open the app. If approved, the item goes straight into the cart. If rejected, the courier moves on without it.

Option 2: Choose a replacement now

Customer experience

At checkout, the customer taps the item and selects a specific replacement from the catalog. Their choice is saved to the order before the shop begins.

For customers who want full control, this option delivers exactly that. At checkout, they tap the item and select a specific replacement from the catalog — their choice is locked in before the shop begins. No notifications mid-order, no decisions under pressure, no surprises at the door.

This speaks directly to what research told us customers valued most: control, transparency, choice, and ease. The ability to pre-select a replacement is the purest expression of all four — the customer has made their decision on their own terms, in their own time, and the order carries it forward without any further input needed. Set it and forget it, exactly as they asked for.

Courier experience

If the original item is out of stock, the courier immediately sees the customer's pre-selected replacement. No message sent, no wait — they pick the replacement and continue shopping without interruption.

Results

Out-of-stock items dropped from 12% to 4% — a 67% reduction in unresolved stockouts.

Reflection

Pre-order decisions can dramatically improve in-order experience — but only when the upfront ask feels proportionate to the benefit.

Getting that balance right was the core design challenge here. The best outcome was a feature most customers would barely notice — because the default did the work for them.
