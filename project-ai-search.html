AI Search: A Natural Language Search for Customers

Overview

Replacing a form-filling experience with a natural conversation.

Grocery shopping is a list — not a search. But our product was forcing customers to search for items one by one, navigate category hierarchies, and scroll through endless product tiles to find things they already knew they needed. AI Search changes that, letting customers describe what they want the way they'd tell a friend: naturally, in their own words, all at once.

Problem

A 25% search conversion rate — and a search experience built for a different era.

Our catalog search was converting at just 25%. Customers were being asked to individually search for each item on their list — a slow, repetitive process that compounds quickly when you're grocery shopping for a full week.

It's the difference between filling out a structured form and having a conversation. When you send someone to the store in real life, you don't give them a keyword — you give them a list. "Grab some milk, eggs, and bread." No categories. No filters. Just what you need.

That gap between how people think and how our search worked was creating real friction — decision fatigue, abandoned sessions, and lost orders.

It's like making someone fill out a form when they'd rather just say what they need.

Goal

Let customers search the way they actually think — in natural language, all at once.

The goal was to close the gap between thought and action. Instead of searching for items one at a time, customers should be able to describe what they need — a full grocery list, a recipe, an occasion — and have the right products surface instantly, in a single interaction.

Critically, this had to be an enhancement, not a disruption. Customers comfortable with the existing search experience shouldn't be forced into a new one.

Text Search

Natural language search — multi-item, multi-category, one interaction.

AI Search allows customers to describe exactly what they need using natural, conversational language. A customer can type "I need milk, fruit, and eggs" or "ingredients for a cheesecake" and receive a structured result set covering everything they asked for — organized by category and ready to add to their basket in one tap.

To encourage this behaviour, the search placeholder rotates through example phrases that model the kind of input the AI understands: "I want to bake a chocolate cake," "I need milk and eggs," "What do I need for tacos?" — nudging customers toward a more natural way of searching from their very first interaction.

Voice Search

Say your list out loud — and get everything back at once.

For customers who want to move even faster, voice search takes natural language to its most direct form. Rather than typing, customers can speak their list or request — "I need milk, fruit, and eggs" or "I want to make a cheesecake" — and the AI pulls up all the relevant items in a single structured result.

It's the closest digital equivalent to telling someone in the store exactly what you need — no friction, no tapping, no category browsing required.

Outcome

An optional, conversational search experience that meets customers where they are.

AI Search launched as a fully opt-in toggle — customers who prefer the existing search experience are never forced into the new one. For those who enable it, the experience adapts to however they want to communicate: typed lists, full sentences, recipe requests, or spoken directly into the app.

Results surface as a structured list organized by category, with every item ready to add to the basket in a single tap. Less friction at the most repetitive part of the shopping experience — and a direct path to improving the search conversion rate that motivated the project in the first place.
