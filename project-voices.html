Overview

Designing across both sides of the world's #1 voice over marketplace.

Voices is the world's leading voice over marketplace, connecting businesses with professional voice talent globally. The platform operates as a two-sided marketplace — serving both the clients who post jobs and the talent who complete them — which means every design decision has to work for two very different audiences at once.

As a Product Designer on a lean team of three, I contributed to the overall UX/UI of the platform across both the client and talent sides. I worked from market research and usage data, facilitated design workshops with designers and stakeholders, supported user testing sessions alongside the UX research team, and collaborated closely with developers throughout the build phase.

I also contributed to Voices' Medium publication as a writer. Read my articles →

Designing

Taking the platform from one service to four — and redesigning every flow to match.

During my time at Voices, I assisted in a company-wide expansion of services. Growing from one service offering to four meant a significant design overhaul — every flow across the platform needed to be reconsidered and rebuilt to accommodate the scale of that growth.

Below is the Job List for the talent side of the platform, which I redesigned. All designs include responsive screen variations for desktop, tablet, and mobile.

Design Systems

Keeping a shared system accessible, consistent, and up to WCAG 2.1 standards.

Working on a small design team of three meant every designer had real ownership of the design system. Keeping it healthy, consistent, and accessible wasn't a specialised role — it was everyone's responsibility.

We used Figma as our primary tool, leveraging its variants feature to build a component library that could scale cleanly across the platform. All components were maintained to WCAG 2.1 accessibility standards, ensuring the product worked for every user — not just the majority.

Discovery

Sitting in on real sessions — and iterating until the design earns its way to hi-fi.

During the discovery phase of any new project, I joined virtual sessions with real users of the platform — watching how they interact, where they hesitate, and what they say when they don't know anyone is listening. That firsthand exposure shapes everything that comes after.

Depending on the project scope, I tested hi-fi or lo-fi designs directly with users. The image below shows sketches tested in lo-fi alongside what was eventually designed and released — a reminder that the final screen is always the result of what got discarded first.

From there, I iterated rapidly through multiple design rounds in parallel with development — working closely with developers during the build phase to inspect designs and ensure they shipped to standard.
