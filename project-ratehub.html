Overview

Helping Canadians make smarter financial decisions — without needing to be a finance expert.

Ratehub.ca is one of Canada's most popular and trusted financial comparison platforms. It helps millions of Canadians compare mortgage rates, credit cards, insurance, and savings products — cutting through the complexity of personal finance so people can make confident decisions without needing a financial advisor.

As the sole Product Designer, I owned design across all business units — from mortgages to credit cards to financial calculators. I partnered closely with stakeholders from ideation through to launch, led the design system, and ran moderated and unmoderated user research throughout. The through-line across every project was the same: make something intimidating feel simple.

Mortgages

Putting the most important information where people could actually see it.

A mortgage is one of the biggest financial decisions most Canadians will ever make — and the page meant to help them wasn't doing the job. Rates were buried below the fold, the layout was heavy, and Ratehub's own insights weren't visible until users had already scrolled past the point where most would give up.

The redesign brought current best rates and key insights above the fold, reduced vertical height significantly, and gave users a clearer picture of their options the moment they arrived. I ran user interviews and usability sessions before launch to validate the approach, and monitored Hotjar post-launch to see how real users responded to the live experience.

Credit Cards

Fewer decisions per scroll — so users could find the right card faster.

Choosing a credit card shouldn't feel like reading a terms and conditions page. The previous homepage packed in too much content too early, pushing the actual card options users came to see further down the page than they needed to be.

The redesign stripped back the content hierarchy and brought more card options into view without sacrificing the context people need to compare. Understanding where users naturally reach a decision point — and designing to that moment — was the key to making the page work.

Calculators

Turning numbers into answers people can actually act on.

Financial calculators are one of the highest-value tools on a platform like Ratehub — but only if they're easy enough to use without a finance degree. I designed a suite of tools covering home affordability, mortgage payoff, and TFSA savings, with the goal of turning complex inputs into clear, human outputs. A number means nothing without context. These tools give users the context they need to take the next step with confidence.
