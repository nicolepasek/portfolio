Overview

A data-dense dashboard designed for clarity — surfacing signal without overwhelming operators.

Operations teams at Just Eat Takeaway needed a single view across courier performance, order health, and regional SLAs. The existing tooling was a patchwork of exported spreadsheets and third-party BI tools with no shared language. This project created a unified internal dashboard that brought the most critical metrics together.

Hierarchy

Surface what needs attention now, before everything else.

Density

Power users need a lot of data on one screen without it feeling overwhelming.

Speed

Decisions happen in real time — the dashboard must load and update fast.

Problem

Ops teams were making decisions from stale, scattered data.

Regional operations managers had no real-time view of their area. They relied on hourly email reports, manual exports, and phone calls to understand live order and courier status. During peak periods this created delays and blind spots — the business couldn't respond quickly to incidents because nobody had a shared, live picture.

Research

One week embedded with the ops team exposed what actually mattered.

Rather than starting with wireframes, I spent a week sitting with the regional ops team during live shifts — observing what they looked for, what they ignored, and what caused them to escalate. This gave me a prioritised list of the eight metrics that genuinely drove decisions, versus the dozens that were available but rarely consulted.

Solution

A KPI-forward layout with progressive disclosure for deeper investigation.

The top of the dashboard showed eight KPI tiles — each with a current value, trend indicator, and threshold alert. Below, users could drill into any metric through expandable chart panels. A persistent alert rail on the right surfaced anomalies that required action, so operators could see at a glance if anything needed immediate attention.

The information architecture was designed so that a manager checking in during a commute and an incident responder deep in a live crisis could both use the same interface effectively.

Outcome

Mean time to incident detection dropped by 22 minutes.

The dashboard was adopted by 94% of eligible ops users within the first month. Post-launch interviews consistently cited the alert rail as the highest-value feature. The project became the foundation for a wider internal tooling redesign that followed.
